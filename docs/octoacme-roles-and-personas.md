# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Clarifies requirements, discusses technical trade-offs and feasibility
- **Project Managers**: Provides estimates, flags dependencies and technical risks
- **UX Designers**: Collaborates on implementation approach, discusses technical constraints
- **Scrum Master**: Raises blockers, participates in ceremonies, provides velocity data
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinates on test strategy, fixes bugs, improves testability

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Provides technical feasibility input and rough effort estimates
- **Planning**: Breaks down work, estimates stories, identifies technical dependencies
- **Execution**: Implements features, writes tests, reviews code, resolves bugs
- **Release**: Supports deployment, monitors production, addresses critical issues
- **Retrospective**: Shares technical insights and suggests process/tooling improvements

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Defines requirements, clarifies acceptance criteria, validates solutions
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on priorities, approves scope changes, reviews timelines
- **UX Designers**: Collaborates on user research, validates designs meet user needs
- **Scrum Master**: Respects team capacity, participates in backlog refinement
- **External Stakeholders**: Translates business needs into product requirements
- **QA/Testing**: Defines success metrics and quality standards

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Defines problem statement and success metrics, approves project charter
- **Planning**: Prioritizes backlog, defines acceptance criteria, validates scope
- **Execution**: Reviews progress, adjusts priorities, makes trade-off decisions
- **Release**: Validates release criteria met, approves go-live
- **Retrospective**: Reviews success metrics, identifies opportunities for next iteration

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Weekly syncs on priorities and scope changes
- **Developers**: Tracks progress, facilitates planning, coordinates dependencies
- **Scrum Master**: Aligns on team process, escalates cross-team blockers
- **UX Designers**: Ensures design milestones align with project timeline
- **External Stakeholders**: Provides status updates, escalates decisions, manages expectations
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinates test planning and release readiness

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Creates project charter, identifies stakeholders, defines timeline
- **Planning**: Facilitates kickoff, creates project plan, manages risk register
- **Execution**: Tracks progress, manages risks, coordinates communication
- **Release**: Manages deployment schedule, coordinates stakeholder communication
- **Retrospective**: Facilitates retrospective, tracks action items, documents learnings

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile processes, remove impediments, and coach the team on Scrum practices. They ensure the team follows agreed-upon processes and continuously improves delivery efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team progress
- Shield the team from external distractions and unplanned work
- Coach team members on agile practices and self-organization
- Track team metrics (velocity, cycle time, burndown) and identify improvement opportunities
- Coordinate with Project Managers on cross-team dependencies

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure process adherence without bureaucracy
- Enable team autonomy and self-organization

### Typical Communication
- Facilitate daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Share sprint metrics and team health indicators
- Escalate persistent blockers to Project Manager
- Coach team members in 1:1s and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Facilitates ceremonies, removes impediments, coaches on agile practices
- **Product Managers**: Helps protect team capacity, provides visibility into delivery pace
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on cross-team dependencies and resource constraints
- **UX Designers**: Ensures design work is integrated into sprint planning and backlog refinement
- **External Stakeholders**: Shields team from ad-hoc requests, channels input through proper channels

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Helps estimate team capacity and initial velocity
- **Planning**: Facilitates sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- **Execution**: Daily facilitation, blocker removal, metrics tracking
- **Release**: Coordinates release activities with team, ensures smooth deployment process
- **Retrospective**: Facilitates retrospectives and tracks improvement action items

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, create interface designs, and validate usability to ensure products are intuitive and delightful. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to balance user needs with technical constraints.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define interaction patterns and information architecture
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria with Product Managers
- Work with Developers on implementation feasibility and design systems
- Validate designs with users and iterate based on feedback

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Advocate for user needs in product decisions
- Maintain design consistency and scalability
- Reduce usability issues and support burden

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design specs and interaction documentation
- Usability test results and improvement suggestions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature definition, validates designs solve user problems
- **Developers**: Provides design specs, discusses technical constraints, reviews implementation
- **QA/Testing**: Defines usability test cases, validates acceptance criteria from UX perspective
- **Scrum Master**: Participates in sprint planning to ensure design work is properly estimated
- **External Stakeholders**: Presents design concepts, gathers feedback on user experience

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Provides early user research insights, helps define success metrics
- **Planning**: Creates design concepts, defines UX acceptance criteria, estimates design effort
- **Execution**: Delivers designs iteratively, reviews implementation, conducts usability tests
- **Release**: Validates final implementation meets design standards
- **Retrospective**: Shares UX insights and suggests process improvements

## External Stakeholder

### Role Summary
External Stakeholders are individuals or groups outside the immediate project team who have an interest in or are affected by the project outcomes. They may include executives, customers, partner teams, legal, compliance, or other business units.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and constraints
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Offer domain expertise and contextual guidance
- Communicate organizational priorities and deadlines
- Validate that outcomes meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with business objectives
- Protect organizational interests and compliance requirements
- Provide timely feedback and approvals
- Communicate needs and expectations clearly

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and status updates
- Approval requests for major decisions
- Escalations for business-critical issues
- Feedback on demos and prototypes

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Primary contact for status updates, approval requests, and escalations
- **Product Managers**: Provides business context, validates product direction and priorities
- **Developers**: May provide technical requirements or constraints (rare, usually through PM/PdM)
- **Scrum Master**: Minimal direct interaction; requests channeled through Project Manager
- **UX Designers**: Reviews designs when business or compliance implications exist

### Project Lifecycle Involvement
- **Initiation**: Approves project charter, defines business constraints and success criteria
- **Planning**: Reviews and approves project scope, timeline, and resource allocation
- **Execution**: Receives regular status updates, provides feedback at milestone reviews
- **Release**: Approves go-live decision, receives release communications
- **Retrospective**: May participate to share organizational learnings or strategic context
